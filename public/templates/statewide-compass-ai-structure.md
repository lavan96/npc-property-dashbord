# Statewide Investor's Compass — AI Structure Template v1.0

> This document defines the mandatory structure, tone, and content requirements for generating a **State-Level Investor's Compass Report**. The AI must follow this template precisely when producing statewide property market analysis reports.

---

## Report Metadata

| Field | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| **Report Title** | `[State/Territory Name] Property Market — Investor's Compass Report` |
| **Scope** | Full state/territory market analysis |
| **Target Length** | 10,000–18,000 words |
| **Tone** | Macro-economic, strategic, institutional-grade |
| **Audience** | Portfolio investors, fund managers, buyers' agents, financial advisors |
| **Data Recency** | Most recent available (state source + date) |

---

## Section 1: Executive Summary

### 1.1 State Investment Climate Summary
- **Length**: 200–500 words
- **Purpose**: Provide a macro-level assessment of the state's property market and investment climate
- **Must Include**:
  - Overall state market rating (e.g., "Growth Phase", "Peak", "Correction", "Recovery")
  - Position in the national property cycle
  - State GDP and economic health summary
  - Population growth trajectory and interstate migration trends
  - Key state-level policy impacts on property investment
  - Top 3 opportunity regions and brief rationale
  - Primary macro risks (1–2 bullet points)
- **Tone**: Strategic, macro-focused, suitable for institutional-level reporting

### 1.2 Key Takeaways
- **Length**: 100–300 words
- **Format**: 5–8 bullet points
- **Must Include**:
  - State-wide median price and annual movement
  - Metro vs regional performance divergence
  - Rental market tightness indicator
  - Interest rate impact assessment
  - Population and migration headline
  - Key infrastructure mega-project
  - Government policy change impacting investors

## Section 2: State Economic Overview

### 2.1 GDP & Economic Output
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - State Gross State Product (GSP) and national share
  - GSP growth rate (annual and trend)
  - Key economic sectors and their contribution to GSP
  - Economic diversification assessment
  - Business confidence indicators
  - Comparison to other states' economic performance
  - Trade exposure (export industries and markets)

### 2.2 Employment & Major Industries
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - State unemployment rate vs national average
  - Employment growth trend (12-month, 5-year)
  - Top 5 employment industries by workforce share
  - Emerging industries and growth sectors
  - Major employers (government, corporate, institutional)
  - Regional employment variation (metro vs regional)
  - Skills shortages and wage growth indicators

### 2.3 Population Growth & Migration
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Current state population
  - Annual population growth rate
  - Natural increase vs net migration breakdown
  - Interstate migration (net gain/loss and from which states)
  - Overseas migration contribution
  - Where population growth is concentrated (growth corridors)
  - Population projections (5-year, 10-year, 20-year)

### 3.1 State-Wide Medians & Activity
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - State-wide median house price
  - State-wide median unit price
  - Year-on-year price movement
  - Total transaction volume (12 months)
  - Market turnover rate
  - Comparison to national medians
  - Price growth relative to inflation and wage growth

### 3.2 Days on Market & Clearance Rates
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - State-wide average days on market
  - Capital city auction clearance rate
  - Clearance rate trend (12-month direction)
  - Private treaty vs auction market split
  - Vendor discount trends
  - What these indicators signal about market conditions

### 3.3 Total Listings Volume
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Total active listings state-wide
  - New listings flow (monthly average)
  - Listings volume vs 5-year average
  - Absorption rate
  - Whether the state is in undersupply or oversupply
  - Regional variation in listing volumes

- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - State housing completions (actual vs target)
  - Building approvals trend
  - Construction sector capacity and constraints
  - Material and labour cost pressures
  - Apartment settlement risk in major CBD markets
  - Developer insolvency trends
  - National Housing Accord implications for the state

### 9.3 Population Growth Corridors
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Designated growth corridors and their boundaries
  - Greenfield vs infill growth allocation
  - Expected population in growth corridors (10-year, 20-year)
  - Infrastructure planned for growth corridors
  - Current property price levels in growth corridors
  - Land banking opportunities and risks
  - Master-planned community developments

## Section 10: Investment Hotspots

### 10.1 Top Opportunity Regions/Suburbs
- **Length**: 200+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Top 5–10 recommended investment locations with justification
  - Mix of capital city and regional recommendations
  - Mix of growth-focused and yield-focused picks
  - Entry price ranges for each recommendation
  - Expected growth trajectory (short-term and medium-term)
  - Risk level for each pick
  - Investor type each pick suits (first-time, experienced, SMSF, etc.)

### 10.2 Hotspot Reasoning & Data
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Data-driven rationale for each hotspot selection
  - Specific metrics supporting the recommendation
  - Catalyst events or triggers expected to drive growth
  - Timeline for expected returns
  - Contrarian picks (areas the mainstream market is undervaluing)

### 10.3 State-Level SWOT Analysis
- **Length**: 200+ words
- **Format**: Structured SWOT for the entire state market

| | Positive | Negative |
|---|----------|----------|
| **Internal** | **Strengths** (4–6 points) | **Weaknesses** (4–6 points) |
| **External** | **Opportunities** (4–6 points) | **Threats** (4–6 points) |

- Each point must reference specific data, regions, or policies
- Include national and global macro factors in Opportunities/Threats

## Formatting Rules

1. **All monetary values** must use Australian dollar format: `$1,234,567`
2. **All percentages** must include sign for growth figures: `+3.5%` or `-1.2%`
3. **All data points** must cite the source and date where possible
4. **Tables** must be properly formatted markdown with alignment
5. **Section headings** must follow the exact hierarchy defined above
6. **Bold key statistics** within paragraph text for scannability
7. **Use bullet points** for lists of 3+ items
8. **State-wide figures** must always be contextualised against national benchmarks
9. **Regional data** must be presented consistently across all sections
10. **Every claim** must be supported by data or clearly marked as assessment/opinion
11. **Abbreviations** must be defined on first use (e.g., GSP, LGA, SA2)
12. **Government policies** must reference the specific legislation or program name
