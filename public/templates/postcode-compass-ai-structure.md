# Postcode/ZIP Code Investor's Compass — AI Structure Template v1.0

> This document defines the mandatory structure, tone, and content requirements for generating a **Postcode-Level Investor's Compass Report**. The AI must follow this template precisely when producing postcode/ZIP code zone analysis reports.

---

## Report Metadata

| Field | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| **Report Title** | `Postcode [XXXX] Investor's Compass Report` |
| **Scope** | Postcode zone analysis (multi-suburb) |
| **Target Length** | 8,000–14,000 words |
| **Tone** | Analytical, comparative, data-rich |
| **Audience** | Property investors, buyers' agents, portfolio strategists |
| **Data Recency** | Most recent available (state source + date) |

---

## Section 1: Executive Summary

### 1.1 Postcode Investment Thesis
- **Length**: 150–400 words
- **Purpose**: Provide a concise, comparative summary of the postcode zone as an investment destination
- **Must Include**:
  - Overall zone investment rating
  - Number of suburbs covered and their collective character
  - Key growth drivers for the zone (2–3 bullet points)
  - Primary risk factors (1–2 bullet points)
  - Aggregated median price context
  - Best-value suburb within the zone (one-line callout)
- **Tone**: Strategic, comparative, suitable for portfolio-level decision-making

### 1.2 Key Takeaways
- **Length**: 100–300 words
- **Format**: 5–7 bullet points
- **Must Include**:
  - Aggregated median price and movement
  - Best and worst performing suburbs within the zone
  - Rental yield range across the zone
  - Population growth trajectory for the zone
  - Key infrastructure project impacting the zone
  - Supply pipeline status

---

## Section 2: Zone Profile

### 2.1 Suburbs Covered
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Complete list of suburbs within the postcode
  - Brief one-line descriptor for each suburb
  - Map reference or geographic arrangement description
  - Total land area of the postcode zone

### 2.2 LGA & Geographic Boundaries
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - LGA(s) the postcode falls within (may span multiple)
  - SA3 and SA4 regions
  - State and federal electorate(s)
  - Neighbouring postcodes
  - Major geographic boundaries (rivers, highways, coastline)

### 2.3 Zone Character & Overview
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Overall character of the zone (residential, mixed-use, coastal, etc.)
  - Socioeconomic positioning
  - Historical development context
  - Key defining features of the zone
  - How the zone is perceived in the broader market

---

## Section 3: Market Overview

### 3.1 Aggregated Medians (Houses/Units)
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Postcode-wide median house price
  - Postcode-wide median unit price
  - Year-on-year movement for each
  - Price range within the zone (cheapest suburb to most expensive)
  - How the zone compares to the broader LGA and metro market
  - Volume of transactions across the zone

### 3.2 Days on Market & Clearance Rates
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Zone-average days on market (houses and units)
  - Auction clearance rate for the zone
  - Vendor discount average
  - Trend direction (tightening or loosening)
  - Comparison to city-wide benchmarks

### 3.3 Stock on Market
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Total active listings in the zone
  - Months of supply calculation
  - New listing flow vs absorption rate
  - Whether the zone is in an undersupply or oversupply position

## Section 5: Price Trends & Growth

### 5.1 Postcode-Wide Historical Trends
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Annualised growth rates: 1-year, 3-year, 5-year, 10-year (zone aggregate)
  - Growth trajectory assessment (accelerating, stable, decelerating)
  - Notable market cycle events (booms, corrections)
  - Current position in the property cycle
  - Suburb-level growth dispersion (which suburbs led, which lagged)

### 5.2 Metro & State Benchmarks
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Zone growth vs metro median growth
  - Zone growth vs state median growth
  - Zone growth vs national median growth
  - Whether the zone is outperforming or underperforming benchmarks
  - Historical benchmark comparison (has the zone always outperformed, or is this recent?)

- **Must Include**:

  - Year-on-year rental growth
  - Rental affordability index for the zone
  - Tenant demand indicators

### 6.3 Vacancy Rates Across Zone
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Zone-average vacancy rate
  - Suburb-level vacancy variation

## Section 7: Demographics & Economics

### 7.1 Population & Growth
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Total zone population
  - Growth rate (annual and 5-year)
  - Migration patterns (where residents are coming from)
  - Age demographics summary
  - Projected growth for the zone

### 7.2 Income & Employment
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Median household income (zone aggregate)
  - Income variation across suburbs within the zone
  - Top employment industries
  - Unemployment rate vs state average
  - Major employers within or near the zone
  - Economic resilience assessment

### 7.3 Household Profile
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Owner-occupier vs renter split (zone average)
  - Predominant household types
  - Average household size
  - How household composition varies across suburbs
  - Implications for investment strategy

- **Must Include**:

### 9.2 Market Diversification Analysis
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:

  - Price segment diversification

### 10.2 Best Value Suburbs Within Postcode
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Ranked list of suburbs by overall investment merit
  - "Best for Growth" suburb pick with rationale
  - "Best for Yield" suburb pick with rationale
  - "Best Value" (undervalued relative to neighbours) suburb pick with rationale
  - Entry-level budget recommendation
  - Premium-segment recommendation

### 10.3 SWOT Analysis
- **Length**: 200+ words
- **Format**: Structured SWOT for the entire zone

| | Positive | Negative |
|---|----------|----------|
| **Internal** | **Strengths** (3–5 points) | **Weaknesses** (3–5 points) |
| **External** | **Opportunities** (3–5 points) | **Threats** (3–5 points) |

- Each point must reference specific suburbs or data where applicable

## Formatting Rules

1. **All monetary values** must use Australian dollar format: `$1,234,567`
2. **All percentages** must include sign for growth figures: `+3.5%` or `-1.2%`
3. **All data points** must cite the source and date where possible
4. **Tables** must be properly formatted markdown with alignment
5. **Section headings** must follow the exact hierarchy defined above
6. **Bold key statistics** within paragraph text for scannability
7. **Use bullet points** for lists of 3+ items
8. **Cross-reference suburbs** within the zone consistently
9. **Every claim** must be supported by data or clearly marked as assessment/opinion
10. **Suburb names** must be consistent throughout (no abbreviations unless established)
