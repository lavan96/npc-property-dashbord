# Suburb Investor's Compass — AI Structure Template v1.0

> This document defines the mandatory structure, tone, and content requirements for generating a **Suburb-Level Investor's Compass Report**. The AI must follow this template precisely when producing suburb analysis reports.

---

## Report Metadata

| Field | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| **Report Title** | `[Suburb Name] Investor's Compass Report` |
| **Scope** | Single suburb analysis |
| **Target Length** | 8,000–15,000 words |
| **Tone** | Authoritative, data-driven, investor-focused |
| **Audience** | Property investors, buyers' agents, financial advisors |
| **Data Recency** | Most recent available (state source + date) |

---

## Section 1: Executive Summary

### 1.1 Suburb Investment Thesis
- **Length**: 150–400 words
- **Purpose**: Provide a concise, high-conviction summary of whether this suburb represents a strong investment opportunity
- **Must Include**:
  - Overall investment rating (e.g., "Strong Buy", "Hold", "Emerging Opportunity")
  - One-line suburb positioning statement
  - Key growth drivers (2–3 bullet points)
  - Primary risk factors (1–2 bullet points)
  - Current median price context vs 5-year trend
- **Tone**: Confident, direct, suitable for executive decision-making

### 1.2 Key Takeaways
- **Length**: 100–300 words
- **Format**: 5–7 bullet points
- **Must Include**:
  - Median price and recent movement direction
  - Rental yield summary
  - Population growth trajectory
  - Supply pipeline status (undersupplied/oversupplied/balanced)
  - Standout risk or opportunity

---

## Section 2: Suburb Profile

### 2.1 Overview & Character
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Suburb description and "feel" (family-friendly, cosmopolitan, coastal, etc.)
  - Distance from CBD and major centres
  - Key landmarks and defining features
  - Historical context (when developed, major changes)
  - Predominant property types (houses, units, townhouses, mix)
  - Typical block sizes and architectural styles

### 2.2 Geographic Context
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Bordering suburbs (north, south, east, west)
  - Proximity to coastline, rivers, or natural features
  - Topography (flat, hilly, elevated)
  - Major road corridors and motorway access
  - Train/metro station proximity

### 2.3 Statistical Areas (SA2/SA3/LGA)
- **Length**: 50+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - SA2 region name and code
  - SA3 region name
  - LGA name
  - State electorate (if relevant)
  - Federal electorate (if relevant)

---

- **Must Include**:
  - Commentary on price positioning relative to LGA and metro averages
  - Price gap analysis (houses vs units)
  - Market sentiment indicators

### 3.2 Market Activity
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Required Table**:

| Metric | Houses | Units |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Total Sales (12 months) | X | X |

---

## Section 4: Price Trends & Growth

### 4.1 Capital Growth History (1/3/5/10yr)
- **Length**: 150+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Annualised growth rates: 1-year, 3-year, 5-year, 10-year
  - Comparison to LGA, metro, and national growth benchmarks
  - Notable peaks, troughs, or inflection points
  - Growth consistency rating (volatile/steady/accelerating)
  - Commentary on where in the property cycle the suburb sits

### 4.2 Price Distribution
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - 25th percentile, median, and 75th percentile prices
  - Price range spread (entry-level to premium)
  - Predominant price bracket
  - What this means for different investor profiles (cash flow vs growth)

### 4.3 Quarterly Movement
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Last 4 quarters of price movement
  - Quarter-on-quarter percentage change
  - Momentum assessment (accelerating, decelerating, flat)

---

## Section 5: Rental Market Deep Dive

### 5.1 Median Rents by Property Type
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Weekly median rents: houses, units, townhouses
  - Rent-to-income ratio for the suburb
  - Rental affordability assessment
  - Year-on-year rental growth rate

### 5.2 Rental Yield Trends
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Current gross yield by property type
  - 3-year and 5-year yield trajectory
  - Yield comparison vs metro average and neighbouring suburbs
  - Net yield estimate (after typical expenses)

### 5.3 Vacancy Rate History
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Current vacancy rate
  - 12-month vacancy trend
  - Comparison to healthy benchmark (2.5–3.5%)
  - Seasonal vacancy patterns if notable

### 5.4 Rental Demand Drivers
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Key tenant demographics (young professionals, families, students, etc.)
  - Proximity to employment hubs
  - Major employers or institutions driving rental demand
  - Infrastructure projects that may boost future rental demand

---

## Section 6: Demographics & Economics

### 6.1 Population & Growth
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Current population estimate
  - Population growth rate (annual, 5-year)
  - Net migration trends (interstate + overseas)
  - Projected population (5-year, 10-year if available)

### 6.2 Age Profile
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Median age
  - Age distribution breakdown (0–14, 15–24, 25–44, 45–64, 65+)
  - Comparison to state/national median age
  - What the age profile means for property demand (e.g., young = rentals, older = downsizers)

### 6.3 Household Income
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Median household income (weekly and annual)
  - Income growth trend
  - Comparison to LGA and metro averages
  - Income-to-price ratio and borrowing capacity implications

### 6.4 Employment Sectors
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Top 5 employment industries
  - Unemployment rate vs state average
  - Major employers in or near the suburb
  - Work-from-home percentage (post-COVID relevance)
  - Economic diversification assessment

### 6.5 Household Composition & Tenure
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Owner-occupier vs renter split (%)
  - Household types (couples with children, lone person, group household)
  - Average household size
  - Implications for investor strategy (high renter % = strong rental market)

- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Nearest train/metro stations and distance

  - Major road and motorway access

  - Airport proximity
  - Planned transport upgrades

### 7.2 Schools & Education
- **Length**: 100+ words
- **Must Include**:

  - School ratings/rankings if available
  - Childcare/early learning centres
  - Tertiary institutions nearby

### 7.3 Shopping & Retail
- **Length**: 50+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Major shopping centres and distance
  - Local village/strip shopping
  - Supermarkets and convenience

### 7.4 Healthcare Facilities
- **Length**: 50+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Nearest hospital and distance
  - Medical centres and GP clinics
  - Specialist services

### 7.5 Recreation & Lifestyle
- **Length**: 50+ words
- **Must Include**:
  - Parks, reserves, and green spaces
  - Sporting facilities
  - Dining and entertainment precincts
  - Beach/waterfront access if applicable

- **Must Include**:

- **Must Include**:

## Section 10: Investment Score & SWOT

### 10.1 Suburb Investment Score
- **Length**: 80+ words
- **Format**: Score out of 100 with category breakdown
- **Scoring Categories**:

| Category | Weight | Score |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Capital Growth Potential | 25% | /25 |
| Rental Yield | 20% | /20 |
| Supply/Demand Dynamics | 15% | /15 |
| Infrastructure & Amenities | 15% | /15 |
| Demographics & Economics | 15% | /15 |
| Risk Profile | 10% | /10 |
| **Overall** | **100%** | **/100** |

### 10.2 SWOT Analysis
- **Length**: 200+ words
- **Format**: Structured SWOT table

| | Positive | Negative |
|---|----------|----------|
| **Internal** | **Strengths** (3–5 points) | **Weaknesses** (3–5 points) |
| **External** | **Opportunities** (3–5 points) | **Threats** (3–5 points) |

- Each point must be specific, data-backed, and actionable

## Formatting Rules

1. **All monetary values** must use Australian dollar format: `$1,234,567`
2. **All percentages** must include sign for growth figures: `+3.5%` or `-1.2%`
3. **All data points** must cite the source and date where possible
4. **Tables** must be properly formatted markdown with alignment
5. **Section headings** must follow the exact hierarchy defined above
6. **Bold key statistics** within paragraph text for scannability
7. **Use bullet points** for lists of 3+ items
8. **Every claim** must be supported by data or clearly marked as assessment/opinion
